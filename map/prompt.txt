Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: master raster for an offline XYZ GIS tile set in a Qt military simulation
Primary request: Create from scratch a fictional coastal operational region seen in a high-resolution satellite mosaic. It must look like a plausible real place while not reproducing any identifiable real-world location.
Scene/background: A roughly 50 km by 50 km temperate-subtropical coastal theater, north at the top. Include rugged forested mountain ridges and exposed granite in the northwest, a reservoir and concrete dam, a branching river system flowing through a broad central valley into a southeastern estuary, tributaries, wetlands, tidal flats, salt marshes, sandy beaches, rocky headlands, offshore islets and reefs. Include terraced farms, orchards, irregular crop fields, dense and sparse woodland, scrub, grassland, villages, one compact town, scattered rural buildings, an industrial zone, quarry, port, rail line, highways, minor roads, mountain switchbacks, tunnels, bridges and power corridors. Add a small airfield, hardened service areas and a few subtle defensive earthworks that remain believable from orbit and do not dominate the landscape.
Subject: The terrain and its interconnected natural and built geographic features, with enough distinct landmarks for tactical navigation.
Style/medium: Photorealistic nadir satellite imagery, natural multispectral color, realistic land-cover textures, crisp but not oversharpened, coherent scale and drainage, fine spatial detail.
Composition/framing: Perfectly vertical orthographic top-down view, square map, north-up. Geography must continue naturally to every edge. No perspective, horizon, frame or inset.
Lighting/mood: Clear midday observation pass, short consistent shadows, high visibility, no clouds and no atmospheric obstruction.
Color palette: Varied natural forest greens, cultivated greens, muted soil browns, granite gray, pale urban gray, blue-green shallow water and deeper navy offshore water.
Materials/textures: Tree canopy variation, rock strata, field boundaries, river sediment, wetland channels, shore surf, concrete, asphalt and roof clusters at realistic satellite scale.
Constraints: Original fictional geography; internally consistent hydrology, roads and settlement placement; useful at 256-pixel GIS tile scale; no text, labels, numbers, coordinates, borders, grid, legend, compass, interface elements, unit markers, logos, trademarks or watermark.
Avoid: Real-world map labels; painterly or illustrated appearance; isometric or oblique view; fantasy terrain; repeated procedural texture; visible tile seams; clouds; fog; night lighting; extreme color grading; blur; shallow depth of field; black bars.
